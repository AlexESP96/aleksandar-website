Psychological support for pressure, emotions, and meaningful change.

You may still be working, leading, studying, caring for others, and keeping everything moving. At the same time, you may feel emotionally exhausted, overwhelmed, disconnected from yourself, or under constant pressure without fully understanding why.

Sometimes people do not need someone to “fix” them. They need space to slow down, understand what they are feeling, and make sense of the emotions, expectations, and patterns that have become difficult to carry alone.

The approach

Emotional depth, with an understanding of real-world pressure.

Support should feel human, grounded, and connected to real life. The aim is to help you understand yourself more clearly, work through difficult emotions with less pressure, and find a healthier, more sustainable way forward.

My work sits at the intersection of psychological support, public mental health, and professional wellbeing. Alongside psychological counseling and suicide prevention experience, I have spent years in international corporate environments. This helps me understand stress not only emotionally, but also in the context people actually live and work in.

For individuals, this can mean support with stress, emotional overload, self-understanding, work pressure, transitions, relationships, or feeling stuck. For organizations, it can mean burnout prevention, workplace wellbeing, leadership pressure, employee support, and psychologically informed workshops.

The goal is not to make everything feel clinical. The goal is to make psychological support useful, emotionally understandable, and connected to the choices people face in everyday life.

Individual support

What individual sessions often create space for.

Sessions are not about forcing quick positivity. They create a structured, human space to understand what is happening, reduce pressure, and make clearer choices.

Putting words to emotions that have felt confusing, heavy, or difficult to explain.
Understanding patterns around stress, relationships, work pressure, burnout, and self-expectations.
Finding practical next steps without dismissing the emotional depth of what you are experiencing.

How I work

Emotionally grounded

We start with what you are feeling, without rushing to label, minimize, or fix it.

Practical and reflective

The work creates space for insight, but also connects that insight to choices, boundaries, communication, and daily life.

Corporate-aware

Pressure is understood in relation to responsibility, performance, relationships, identity, and professional environments.

Evidence-informed

Psychological knowledge guides the work without making it cold, rigid, or overly academic.
