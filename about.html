About

A grounded, evidence-based approach to everyday pressure.

I am a psychologist with a strong interest in mental health at work, human behavior, and practical support for people navigating pressure, uncertainty, and professional demands.

My work combines academic training, research experience, and years of exposure to organizational environments. I aim to make psychological knowledge useful, structured, and accessible without making support feel cold or clinical.

Professional background

Psychology, research, and the realities of modern work.

I am currently completing a PhD in Psychology at the Universitat de València, with research focused on employee wellbeing, burnout, inclusive employment, leadership, and workplace mental health.

I bring more than 5 years of experience in industry through my work with VeraSafe, where I have worked closely with international, remote-first teams and people-related processes. This gives me a practical understanding of pressure, performance, communication, decision-making, and the everyday challenges professionals face.

I have also worked directly in support-focused roles, including more than 1 year as a psychological counselor and 4 years as a suicide prevention officer. These experiences shaped the way I approach sensitive conversations: with structure, care, and respect for the person in front of me.

I speak Serbian, English, Spanish, and conversational Italian, and I try to adapt to clients linguistically whenever possible. I know how important it can be to express yourself in your native or most comfortable language, especially when discussing pressure, emotions, and personal experiences.

I work with individuals and organizations that want support that is calm, structured, and grounded in psychological science. My style is direct, warm, and practical: the goal is not to overcomplicate your life, but to help you understand what is happening and make better choices from there.
